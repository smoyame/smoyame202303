About Me

From 8 year-olds filling out insurance forms to a parent who can't find their reading glasses. I believe there is no typical person, so there is no typical way to approach design.

The most authentic interaction is imperfect—full of nicks, bumps, and split ends. You stutter, you mis-click, you typo. Design that considers all peoples is design that allows itself to behave like this, too—to be that authentic—while forgiving itself and others in the end.

It always keeps that 8 year-old in mind.
